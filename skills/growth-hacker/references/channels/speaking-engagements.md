# Speaking Engagements

## Definition
Securing time on stage at events your prospects attend.

> *"Teaching sells."* — Dan Martell, Clarity

## When it works
- **B2B/enterprise** where prospects pay to attend industry conferences.
- Consumer too (Martell's Clarity), but harder.
- Particularly good when you have **credentials** (popular blog, prior startup exit, recognized expertise).
- Phase II/III when you have a story worth telling.

## When it doesn't
- Phase I with no proof points.
- Audiences that don't overlap with your buyers.
- When you don't have one polished talk you can repeat.

## Tactics

### Pitch reverse (Steve Barsh, PackLate)
**Don't pitch your topic.** Email the organizer asking what topics they want covered, *then* craft a pitch hitting their list.

### Three event tiers + lead times
- **Premier** (national/international, **6–12 months out**)
- **Regional** (2–4 months)
- **Local** (1–3 months)

Plan accordingly — premier slots fill very early.

### Build credibility free
At co-working spaces, nonprofits, smaller events. Refine talks, build reputation. Organizer world is small — speakers get invited based on **prior gigs**.

### Limited canon
Dan Martell has only **1–2 talks**; he tweaks them per audience but never builds a custom one. *"Each slide is essentially a 7-minute story with beginning/middle/end"* so a 60-min talk → 20-min by removing slides.

### Open every talk
With: *why am I important enough to give this? What value can I offer you?* Self-introduce to control framing — don't let the organizer's bio define you.

### Story-shaped content
What you're doing, why, how you got there. Audiences forget bullet points; they remember stories.

### Leverage every talk
- **Record + post clips** (one talk to 250 people → thousands online).
- **Rand Fishkin (Moz) tweets slides before every presentation.**
- **Martell asks for "divided attention"** — Twitter handle on every slide, ask audience to tweet quotes during the talk.
- **Always end with a CTA** (mailing list, link).

### Speaker dinners
If the conference doesn't schedule one, **schedule it yourself** — often the most valuable part of a conference.

### Use attendee lists
To **pre-arrange post-talk meetings** with high-priority prospects.

## $1k / 1-week test

1. Apply to **3 local meetups + co-working spaces**.
2. Give one free talk.
3. **Record it.**
4. Post clips.
5. Pitch a **regional event with the recording** as your proof.
6. Track CTA conversion.

## Key metrics
- Engagements landed
- Audience size
- **CTA completions per talk**
- Follow-up meetings booked
- Downstream signups/deals

## Pitfalls
- **Custom-building a talk for every event** — unsustainable.
- **No CTA → no measurable downstream.**
- **Ignoring the speaker's dinner.**
- **Not recording.** Every talk is permanent leverage — only if you record it.

## Companies + their plays
- **Clarity (Dan Martell)** — 1–2 talks, repeated, with audience tweet-amplification.
- **Moz (Rand Fishkin)** — pre-tweeted slides as engagement engine.
- **Flowtown / Spheric** — Martell's prior credentials, leveraged for first big talk.
