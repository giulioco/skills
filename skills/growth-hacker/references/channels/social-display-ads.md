# Social & Display Ads

## Definition
Banner ads on web sites (display) and feed-based ads on social platforms (Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, reddit, Tumblr, Instagram, TikTok). Combined ~$15B/year industry at the time of the book; much larger now.

## When it works
- **Any stage** — bidding granularity supports tiny test budgets.
- **Display** for direct response or branding to a broad audience.
- **Social** for **demand generation** (vs. SEM's demand harvesting) — building affinity with people not yet searching.

## When it doesn't
- When you push direct-response on social platforms where users are entertaining themselves; conversion-tracking-only thinking fails on social.
- When your content isn't strong enough to be worth amplifying — *"paid is only as good as the content you put behind it."*
- When the platform is saturated with your competitors all bidding up the same audience.

## Tactics

1. **Spy first** with MixRank and Adbeat to see competitor ads and placements; Alexa/Quantcast for site demographics.
2. **Display ad networks:** Google Display Network (>4B daily page views, 80% of online audience), Advertising.com, TribalFusion, ValueClick, Adblade.
3. **Niche networks:** The Deck (web creatives, one ad per page), BuySellAds (self-service).
4. **Direct ads:** email small bloggers offering ~$500/mo for a banner. Noah Kagan used this at Mint and got thousands of signups.
5. **Test ads in groups of 5** — Mike Colella (Adbeat) kills any with conversion <10%; surveys landing-page opt-ins to refine messaging.
6. **Native ad platforms:** Outbrain, Sharethrough — promote your content on Forbes/Vice/etc.
7. **Indirect response on social** (Nikhil Sethi, Adaptly): build audience first, convert later. Put paid behind organic-reach content. Airbrake spent $15 on Twitter promoting one content piece, got hundreds of organic retweets and tens of thousands of visits.
8. **Platform plays:**
   - **Twitter** — paid ads tied to real-time events (sportswear during the Super Bowl).
   - **Facebook/Instagram** — granular interest/page/connection targeting (Gabriel once targeted only his wife).
   - **reddit** — controversial or funny ads in subreddits.
   - **LinkedIn** — job-title targeting for B2B.

## $1k / 1-week test

Pick **one** of:
- **(a)** $500 on Facebook with 5 ad variants targeting 1 narrow interest cluster.
- **(b)** $500 boosting your single best content piece on Twitter/Facebook + native distribution via Outbrain.
- **(c)** $500 direct-buying banners on 2–3 small niche blogs your customer reads.

Hold $200–500 for landing-page tweaks. Measure CTR, CPC, CPA, conversion rate per ad/landing combo.

## Key metrics
- CTR, CPC, CPA, conversion rate per ad/landing combo
- For social/indirect: engagement rate, follower growth, organic-share multiplier on paid content, email-list growth

## Pitfalls
- **Treating social ads as direct response.** *"The burden of success is on the advertiser, not the platform."*
- **Retargeting can feel creepy** if data is sensitive (e.g., medical, dating).
- **Don't try to grow today the way Zynga grew on Facebook in 2009** — early-platform arbitrage windows close. Find the next emerging platform instead.
- **Aggregate metrics hide channel-level failures.** Always segment by ad set + creative.

## Indirect response for content (Nikhil Sethi, Adaptly)
- Build audience with content first.
- Put paid behind your *best* organic content to amplify reach.
- Convert later through email or product engagement.
- *"With social, it's word of mouth on crack."*

## Companies + their plays
- **Dollar Shave Club** — one $5,000 video reached the same audience Gillette did with $60M/year in marketing.
- **Warby Parker** — try-on photos posted to social by customers as built-in social experience.
- **CareOne** — social-sourced leads completed signups at **680% higher rates** and made first payment **732% better** than non-social leads.
- **Mint** — Noah Kagan paid bloggers $500 each pre-launch.
- **Airbrake** — $15 promoted tweet → tens of thousands of visits.
