# Content Marketing

## Definition
Building an audience through publishing — typically a company blog plus infographics, ebooks, mini-courses. Compounds across SEO, PR, email, business development, and community.

## When it works
- Your buyers spend time online and consume industry content.
- You have **unique data or genuine expertise** to share.
- You can wait **6+ months** for traffic to build.
- Particularly powerful for **B2B SaaS** and consumer products with stigma to remove.

## When it doesn't
- You can't produce quality consistently.
- Your audience doesn't read.
- You expect blog traffic in month 1 — Unbounce had **<800 monthly visits after 6 months**.

## Tactics

1. **Start before you launch.** Unbounce's first hire was a full-time blogger; they blogged a year before they had a product and **launched with a 5,000-person email list**.

2. **Write about customer problems.** Kill writer's block by mining the pain points of your target user (Unbounce → landing page optimization, PPC; OkCupid → "Exactly What to Say In Your First Message").

3. **Be controversial when grounded in data.** OkCupid posts like "How Your Race Affects the Messages You Get" took a month each to write but used proprietary user data and got coverage from CNN, Rachel Ray, NYT.

4. **Lead-magnet content for email capture.** Unbounce released *101 Landing Page Optimization Tips* and the "Noob Guide to Online Marketing" infographic (tens of thousands of downloads, **still shared once an hour a year later**).

5. **Hand-distribute early.** Ping influencers on Twitter for feedback. Answer Quora questions in your space. Do guest posts after ~3 months of building your own blog.

6. **Maintain a content calendar.** Frequency + consistency matter more than perfection.

7. **Infographics share ~20× more** than typical posts.

8. **Use customer-mined language** — see `activation.md` on language/market fit.

## $1k / 1-week test

1. Publish 3 deep posts answering specific customer questions.
2. Publish 1 infographic ($300–500 from a designer).
3. Spend ~$200 boosting them via social ads or native distribution.
4. Hand-distribute via Twitter/Quora/Reddit.
5. **Measure email signups, not pageviews.**

This test is more about pipeline than traffic — content is a 6–12 month channel; the 1-week test confirms you can actually produce + distribute, not that the channel is working.

## Key metrics
- **Email-list growth** (the canonical leading indicator)
- Unique monthly visitors
- Social shares
- Organic search rankings on target topics
- Downstream referral traffic to product
- Backlinks to content (ranks SEO compound)

## Pitfalls
- **No silver bullet** — six months of bi-weekly posts before payoff is normal.
- **Doing it without a CTA / email capture** wastes the audience.
- **Outsourcing soulless content fails** — quality matters; writers without domain expertise produce content nobody shares.
- **Optimizing for traffic instead of conversion.** A 10k-visit post with 0 signups is worse than a 1k-visit post with 100.

## Marketing assets vs. ads (Dharmesh Shah)
Ads stop the moment you stop paying. Content keeps working. This applies to content marketing, SEO, and engineering as marketing — all three are "build once, harvest forever" channels with high upfront cost and compounding payoff.

## Companies + their plays
- **Unbounce** — blogged a year before product, **20,000 monthly visits at launch**, 5,000-person email list pre-launch.
- **OkCupid / OkTrends** — controversial data posts → **#1 for "online dating"** within ~1 year, drove inflection in Quantcast traffic.
- **Moz, HubSpot** — content as spine of inbound (HubSpot's 50,000+ leads/month).
- **Fred Wilson, Brad Feld, Mark Suster** — VC blogs as personal-brand traction.
- **Buffer, Groove, Baremetrics** — radical-transparency blogs (revenue, churn, decisions in public) → outsized share-of-voice.
