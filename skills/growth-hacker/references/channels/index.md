## Quick channel-decision matrix (Aatif Awan, LinkedIn VP of Growth)

| User behavior | Channels to explore |
|---|---|
| Are people using **search** to find a solution? | SEO / SEM |
| Do existing users share via **word of mouth**? | Virality / referral programs |
| Does having more users **improve the experience**? | Virality (network effects) |
| Are your target users on **another platform**? | Existing Platforms / BD |
| Do users have a **high LTV**? | Paid acquisition (SEM, Social Ads, Offline Ads) |

## Three categories of channels (Mares/Weinberg/Chen/Currier)

| Category | Channels |
|---|---|
| **Viral / Word-of-Mouth** | Viral Marketing, Community Building, Existing Platforms (some uses), Embedded widgets |
| **Organic** | SEO, PR, Content Marketing, Email, Speaking, Trade Shows (some uses), Offline Events |
| **Paid** | SEM, Social & Display Ads, Offline Ads, Affiliate, Sponsorships |

Pick across categories — most successful companies have one dominant channel from one category and a strong support channel from another.

## The Critical Path Test

Before brainstorming, write down your **single Traction Goal** (1,000 paying customers, 10% market share, $1M ARR, etc.). Then for every channel, ask:

> "Does this channel have enough volume to plausibly move me toward that goal in the next 6 months?"

Channels that fail this volume test go in column C (long-shot) automatically. This kills "do what's comfortable" energy on day one.
