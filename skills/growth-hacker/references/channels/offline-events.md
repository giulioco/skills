# Offline Events

## Definition
Sponsoring/running gatherings — small meetups → regional conferences → giant shows like Salesforce's Dreamforce (>100k).

## When it works
- **Long-cycle enterprise sales** (Enservio).
- Products serving a community with shared interests (MicroConf).
- Phase I (Eric Ries's Lean Startup launch), Phase II/III (Salesforce-scale).

## When it doesn't
- Products with **scattered/horizontal customer bases** (HitTail).
- Self-serve consumer with no in-person hook.

## Tactics

### Conference-specific stunt (Twitter at SXSW 2007)
**$11k on flat-panel screens in hallways** + a **"text 'join sxsw' to 40404"** feature that auto-followed ambassadors. Tweets jumped from **20k → 60k+/day**; won SXSW Web Award. *"About the only money Twitter's ever spent on marketing."*

### Run your own conference (Eric Ries, Startup Lessons Learned)
Test demand first by emailing readers; sell tickets through your blog and partner blogs. **One-day SF event** minimized speaker logistics. **Live-streamed to meetup groups in 20+ cities** — those meetups became the Lean Startup Circle and kept the book on bestseller lists.

### Lavish executive event (Enservio, Claims Innovation Summit)
Ritz Carlton, Dove Mountain. Did **NOT** make it feel like a sales pitch — pulled in **consulting-firm leaders, industry execs, hot-startup founders as speakers**. Used speakers as bait for the actual prospect-executives. Now an annual industry fixture.

### MicroConf (Rob Walling)
**High-priced tickets to keep attendee quality high**; intentionally small to allow speakers + attendees to actually meet; speakers eat lunch with attendees and join roundtables. First-year challenges: FB ads + AdWords didn't work — **only relational sales did**.

### Mini-conference test ($500 budget)
Three local founders giving short talks at a university lecture hall (free if educational). Or **unconference**: attendees suggest topics, vote, hold roundtables.

### Parties (Evite + Yelp)
- Evite handled invites for a Mahir Cagri party — exposed brand to target users.
- **Yelp threw "Yelp Elite" parties** in new cities, RSVP'd elites first with free food + swag — built local network density before launching the city.

### Meetups (Seth Godin / Linchpin)
**10,000 attendees through blog-organized city meetups.** Nick Pinkston: 60-person Hardware Startup Meetup, $70 in pizza, now 2,600 members.

## $1k / 1-week test

Pick one of:
- **(a)** Mini-conference at a free university lecture hall: 3 invited speakers, registration via your blog, $500 in food, livestream.
- **(b)** Sponsor an existing meetup ($200) with a 10-min talk slot.
- **(c)** Throw a local "drinks for [your-segment]" night, $500 bar tab, invite 30 prospects.

## Key metrics
- Attendees
- **Ticket sell-through (proxy for value)**
- # qualified prospects engaged
- NPS of attendees
- Downstream pipeline (deals/signups in 60 days)

## Pitfalls
- **Conferences sold by pure ads** — only relational/word-of-mouth fills early ones (Walling's first MicroConf).
- **Trying to throw an event for a horizontally-scattered customer base** — you can't get critical mass.
- **No follow-up plan** — events without sequenced follow-up lose 90% of the value.
