# Unconventional PR

## Definition
Anything engineered to generate disproportionate media coverage outside the normal press-pitch flow. Three flavors:

1. **Publicity stunts** — one-off spectacles engineered for coverage.
2. **Viral videos** — content that propagates because it's shareable.
3. **Customer appreciation** — being so awesome to existing customers that they generate buzz.

David Hauser at Grasshopper hired *two full-time employees* dedicated to customer-appreciation alone.

## When it works
- Less crowded than traditional PR — *"nearly every company attempts traditional PR, but few focus on stunts."*
- Any phase, but stunts are especially powerful for **unknown launches**.
- Brand-led consumer products benefit most.

## When it doesn't
- B2B or regulated industries where dignity matters.
- When you can't be creative or extraordinary — half-hearted stunts look desperate.
- Most stunts flop. Have a process, expect a hit rate well under 50%.

## Tactics

### Publicity stunts
- **Half.com** — renamed the town of Halfway, Oregon to "Half.com" for one year. Hired residents. Launched on the Today Show with the mayor. NYT, PBS, WSJ coverage. **40 million impressions** at $70k cost + 2 hires = $0.00175 CPI.
- **WePay** — at PayPal's developer conference, placed a 600-pound block of ice with frozen accounts inside reading *"PayPal freezes your accounts: Unfreeze your money."* Capitalized on PayPal's controversy at PayPal's own event. **Thousands of signups.**
- **DuckDuckGo** — bought a billboard in Google's backyard advertising privacy. Used the billboard itself as the press hook (USA Today, Wired). **Doubled userbase.**
- **Richard Branson / Virgin** — outlandish press conferences (dress like a woman, drive a tank through streets) turn boring launches into national headlines.
- **Grasshopper "Grasshopper 5000"** — sent chocolate-covered grasshoppers to 5,000 influencers with a video about entrepreneurship. Fox News + Guy Kawasaki/Kevin Rose tweets, 200k YouTube views, 150+ blog posts. **3,000% increase in Twitter/Facebook visitors.** Cost $65,000.
- **Chargify** — hired a $3,000 stuntman in a green bull costume to roam SXSW 2010 instead of paying $10–15k as a sponsor.

### Viral videos
- **Blendtec "Will It Blend?"** — CEO blends rake, golf balls, iPhone. **iPhone video: 8M+ views. Sales up 500%+.**
- **Dollar Shave Club** — *"Our Blades Are F**king Great"* launch video. **12,000 customers in 2 days**, 31k FB shares, ranked #3 for Google "shave" via 1,500+ links. Featured in Forbes, WSJ, Wired.
- **Grasshopper "New Dork" video** — name-checked Mashable/TechCrunch so they self-promoted. **1M+ views.**

### Customer appreciation
- **Hipmunk (Alexis Ohanian)** — chipmunk luggage tags + handwritten notes to first several hundred Twitter mentioners. T-shirts, stickers. Mother's Day Giveaway: $500 spent, hundreds of submissions. *"Chipmunked"* Facebook profile pictures by an artist — **500 requests in <1 hour, +350% Facebook fans.**
- **reddit (Alexis again)** — free t-shirts with the reddit alien, personal emails to early users.
- **Grasshopper (David Hauser)** — Skittles, homemade cookies, Starbucks gift cards, books, handwritten notes. **Two FTEs dedicated to delight.**
- **Zappos** — classifies customer service as marketing investment. Won't track average call time. Helps customers order pizza, exchange workout clothes for fryers. Free next-day shipping + free returns.

### Contests
- **Shopify Build A Business** — six-figure prize. **1,900 new customers and $3.5M in platform sales** in last contest year.
- **Dropbox Dropquest** — annual scavenger hunt; **half a million participants** in year one, rewarded with swag and free Dropbox for life.

## $1k / 1-week test

**Customer appreciation:** Send handwritten notes + small gift to your first 50 users for <$500. Track Twitter/social mentions and 30-day retention delta.

**Stunts:** Brainstorm 10 ideas tied to a current news/industry theme; cheapest variant is usually a creative billboard placement, single-day giveaway, or themed mail-drop to 100 influencers ($500–1000).

**Video:** Produce one short, highly opinionated video. Seed via Twitter to space-adjacent influencers. Track views + organic share velocity in the first 24 hours.

## Key metrics
- Press coverage volume
- Social mentions
- Signup spikes during/after stunt
- Referral traffic from earned media
- Retention/word-of-mouth lift from appreciation gestures
- **Cost-per-impression** is the key benchmark for stunts (Half.com: $70k for 40M = $0.00175 CPI)

## Pitfalls
- **Stunt unrelated to product positioning** (no narrative for the press).
- **Stunt with no built-in story angle** for reporters — they'll skip you.
- **One-shot mentality** vs. systematic program (Hauser's two FTEs are the model).
- **Customer appreciation that feels mass-produced** kills the goodwill.
- **Trying to do this consistently as your *only* channel** — hit rate is too low.
