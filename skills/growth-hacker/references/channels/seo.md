# SEO

## Definition
Improving organic search rankings to drive traffic. Two strategies:
- **Fat-head:** rank for short, high-volume terms describing your category.
- **Long-tail:** rank for many specific, lower-volume queries that aggregate to **~70% of all searches**.

## When it works
- When **search demand exists** for your category.
- **Long-tail works at any stage** — startups with little authority can rank for niche queries.
- **Compounds over time;** unlike paid, traffic persists when you stop spending.
- Moz says **85% of their customers come via inbound channels.**

## When it doesn't
- Brand-new categories with no search demand (Rand Fishkin's Uber example: nobody was searching "alternatives to taxi cabs I can hire via my phone").
- Trying to outrank entrenched competitors on fat-head terms with thousands of links.
- Fast-moving startups that need traction in <6 months — SEO is a 6–12 month payoff.

## Tactics

1. **Fat-head:** use Google Keyword Planner to size demand; **validate with paid AdWords first** — if SEM doesn't convert on a term, SEO won't either. Use Google Trends to assess seasonality and geo. Use Open Site Explorer to gauge link difficulty against competitors. Then orient site titles/headers around the term and earn backlinks anchored to it.

2. **Long-tail:** produce volume. Patrick McKenzie pays freelancers $3.50 per term (e.g. "Owls of East Asia bingo") to write a unique article + landing page; over 3 years one such page returned **~$60**. Bundle them under category subpages ("plants and animals" → "dog breeds bingo," etc.).

3. **Geographic long-tail:** "recently foreclosed homes in Queens, New York City" vs. just "foreclosed homes."

4. **Automation / data exhaust:** expose data your product naturally generates. Names Database (Gabriel's exit) auto-generated profile pages from data behind a login wall and sent to search engines. **Yelp, TripAdvisor, Wikipedia all built on this.**

5. **Link building:** PR mentions; remarkable content (infographics, slideshows, original research); guest posts; widgets that embed your link.

6. **Site search competitor recon:** `site:competitor.com` to count their landing pages; Alexa to compare share-of-traffic-from-search across competitors.

## $1k / 1-week test

- **~$300** on AdWords against 10 candidate fat-head terms to see which convert.
- **~$500** on freelance writers (Upwork) to produce 20–30 long-tail articles + landing pages.
- Watch which start ranking over **4–8 weeks** (this is a longer-cycle test than other channels).

## Key metrics
- Rankings for target terms
- Organic sessions
- Organic conversions
- Links acquired
- Domain authority (Moz/Ahrefs)
- Share of traffic from search

## Pitfalls
- **Don't buy links** — black-hat tactics can win short-term but invite penalty.
- **Don't waste effort on terms with "free" in them** — visitors won't pay.
- **Don't pursue terms where you can't realistically reach page one** — ~90% of clicks happen on page 1.
- **Treat SEO as a 6–12 month payoff,** not weekly.
- **Don't auto-generate low-value pages** — Google will penalize. Auto-generate only if the page actually serves the user.

## The "ephemeral pages" trap
If your product generates pages that are time-bound (apartment listings, event listings, daily deals), and those pages get indexed, they'll 404 or disappear later — degrading your domain authority.

**Rule:** noindex ephemeral pages and exclude them in robots.txt. Reserve indexable pages for evergreen content (neighborhood guides, how-to articles, seasonal pages).

## Companies + their plays
- **Moz** — SEO software; content + free tools as the spine.
- **Bingo Card Creator / Patrick McKenzie** — $3.50 → $60 per page over 3 years, pure long-tail.
- **Names Database** — auto-generated long-tail profile pages, sold for $10M.
- **OkCupid** — went from invisible to **#1 for "online dating"** within ~12 months of starting OkTrends blog.
- **Yelp, TripAdvisor, Wikipedia** — data-exhaust SEO at scale.
