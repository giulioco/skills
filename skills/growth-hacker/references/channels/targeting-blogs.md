# Targeting Blogs

## Definition
Reach prospects on the third-party blogs they already read — by getting embedded in posts, banners, sponsorships, content partnerships, or by submitting to link-sharing communities (Hacker News, reddit, Product Hunt, Inbound.org).

## When it works
- Your prospects congregate on a few small blogs you can co-opt.
- Phase I/II — when you need the first wave of customers in a crowded online market.
- When you can afford ~$500 per small-blog sponsorship.

## When it doesn't
- Audience is offline.
- Your prospects don't read blogs in your category.
- You can't justify the per-blog spend.

## Tactics

- **Embeddable badges** (Mint). Provide copy-paste embed code à la YouTube. **600 blogs displayed Mint's "I want Mint" badge** → 50,000 signups + SEO boost from inbound links.
- **Sponsoring small personal blogs.** Noah Kagan emailed bloggers with the subject line *"Can I send you $500?"* — pitched a small ad. Drove **10,000+ pre-registrations** for Mint.
- **Content partnerships with bigger sites.** Mint × The Motley Fool — each contributed content to the other; exposed Mint to ~3M readers.
- **Bundles for a target blog (AppSumo).** Build the offer first, *then* pitch. Noah built a productivity bundle specifically for Lifehacker before reaching out — Lifehacker couldn't say no to something pre-built for their audience.
- **Link-sharing communities.** Dropbox's HN-launched demo video → 10,000 signups, then trended on Digg. Filepicker.io's HN front-page demo: 10k visits in 4 hrs, 460 concurrent, 12k demo users in a day, **500+ developer signups**, 5,000+ files managed.

## $1k / 1-week test

1. Identify 5 small niche blogs via YouTube/Delicious/StumbleUpon/Twitter/Google Alerts/Social Mention.
2. Email each with a *"Can I send you $500?"* subject; offer one a $500 sponsorship and run a co-branded giveaway.
3. Submit a launch demo to Hacker News + a relevant subreddit on day 1.

## Key metrics
- Click-throughs by blog
- Signups per sponsorship
- Cost per signup
- Badge embed count
- HN/Reddit referral spike + 7-day retention from those signups

## Pitfalls
- **Going only after huge blogs** (rejection-heavy, expensive).
- **Posting to link-sharing communities without focused product-fit** — audience won't convert.
- **Skipping the "exclusive offer" hook** in pitches — generic asks get ignored.

## Companies + their plays
- **Mint:** badges + $500 sponsorships + Motley Fool content partnership + 600 blog embeds.
- **AppSumo:** Lifehacker pre-built bundle.
- **Dropbox, Quora, reddit, Codecademy, Gumroad, Filepicker.io:** Hacker News launches.
