# Offline Ads

## Definition
TV, radio, magazines, newspapers, yellow pages, billboards, transit, direct mail, infomercials. Still bigger than online ad spend in absolute dollars.

## When it works
- When your target demographic concentrates in a specific medium.
- **Direct mail** for tightly targetable lists.
- **Local print** for hyperlocal launches.
- **Radio** for time-sensitive offers.
- **Billboards** for event awareness or symbolic statements.
- **Infomercials** for visual products needing demo time (workout equipment, body care, household, health, work-from-home).

## When it doesn't
- Buyer is under 30 and you're advertising in newspapers.
- You can't track ROI.
- Your product needs a clickable CTA on a highway.

## Tactics

1. **Match demographics first** — request the ad kit / audience prospectus from any outlet.
2. **Buy remnant inventory** — leftover slots near print deadlines, **up to 90% off rate card**. Tim Ferriss recommends agencies like Manhattan Media or Novus Media for national magazine remnants.
3. **Track with unique URLs and codes** — flyers link to yourcompany.com/flyer, magazine ad uses "WIRED" coupon code, signup form asks "How did you hear about us?" (Jason Cohen at Smart Bear).
4. **Direct mail:** buy lists at **~$100/1000 names**, use handwritten envelopes, self-addressed return envelope, one clear CTA, bulk-mail postal pricing.
5. **Local print/flyers:** cheap, fine for early traction even if unscalable. **InstaCab hired cyclists** to hand business cards to people hailing taxis in SF.
6. **Billboards:** contact Lamar, Clear Channel, or CBS Outdoor. **$700–2,500/mo in cheap locations; $30–100k Times Square.** DuckDuckGo: $7,000 for a SoMa billboard calling out Google's privacy practices — userbase doubled that month plus *Wired*, *USA Today*, Business Insider press.
7. **Radio:** priced on CPP (cost per point = cost to reach 1% of listeners). $500–1,500/week local, $4–8k major market. 30-sec voice spot production: $300–500.
8. **TV:** Local spots $5–50 per 1,000 viewers, often more workable than national. Use animation or film students to cut production cost. **Always negotiate — no rate cards.**
9. **Infomercials:** $50–500k production. Best practice: **pre-test messaging via radio ads before recording the long-form spot.** P90X: **$400M in sales** primarily via infomercial.

## $1k / 1-week test

Pick one of:
- **(a)** Single radio spot in a small local market ($300 production + $400–500 airtime) with a unique URL/code.
- **(b)** Remnant ad in a small trade magazine.
- **(c)** 1,000-piece direct mail drop with handwritten envelopes.

Track unique URL/code redemptions for 14 days post-airing.

## Key metrics
- Coupon-code redemptions
- Unique-URL visits
- "How did you hear about us?" tags
- Lift in branded search
- Geographic conversion deltas

## Pitfalls
- **Jason Cohen's warning:** *"I have zero ability to predict what's going to work."* Small piddly magazines sometimes outperform big circulation; ROI decays unpredictably over months.
- **Test then commit; don't sign long contracts before a small test confirms.**
- **Don't skip tracking codes** — without unique URLs/codes you can't attribute anything.
