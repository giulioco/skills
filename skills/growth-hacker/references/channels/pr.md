# Public Relations (PR)

## Definition
Earned coverage from traditional media — newspapers, magazines, TV, large online publications.

## When it works
- Phase II/III when you have newsworthy milestones (funding, launches, partnerships, usage records, original research).
- Phase I in modest form: industry blogs, HARO, Twitter to reporters.
- *"In the best scenario, PR can vault you from being unheard of to a market leader essentially overnight."*

## When it doesn't
- No newsworthy milestone.
- No angle that makes readers want to *do* something.
- B2B with no consumer story (consider Trade Shows / BD instead).

## How media works (Ryan Holiday)
Stories filter **up**: Hacker News/reddit → TechCrunch/Lifehacker → NYT/CNN. **Don't pitch CNN directly** — pitch the smaller blogs that TechCrunch reads. Smaller sites legitimize newsworthiness for bigger audiences.

Ad-supported blogs **want** your story (more page views = more ad revenue). *"When Business Insider writes about you, you are doing them the favor."*

**DonorsChoose.org example:** local outlets → Newsweek → an Oprah staffer saw the Newsweek piece → named one of Oprah's favorite things 2010 → Gates Foundation sponsorship.

## Tactics

- **Get reporter attention with milestones:** raising money, launching, breaking a usage barrier, PR stunt, big partnership, special industry report. Bundle smaller announcements together.
- **Pitch templates work.** Jason Baptiste's PadPressed pitch: short, exclusive offer, demo links, contact info, product giveaway offer.
- **Avoid "wall of text"** (Jason Kincaid critique). Be succinct, make timing clear.
- **Craft an angle.** *"Satisfaction is a non-viral emotion"* (Holiday) — make readers want to do something. *Wall Street* book pitched as "stock market is rigged" rather than "high-frequency trading explained" got far more press.
- **Holiday's pitch template:** *"I loved your post on [similar topic that did a lot of traffic]. I was going to give this to our publicist, but I thought I'd come to you with the exclusive…"* Offer to draft something at their average word count.
- **Starting out:** HARO (Help A Reporter Out) — answer source requests for a mention. Offer commentary on industry stories. **Twitter** — most reporters have small follower counts, easier to stand out (DuckDuckGo got into Time Magazine's Top 50 sites 2011 via Twitter relationship).
- **Amplify:** submit to Digg/reddit/HN, share on social with paid amplification, email influencers for comment, ping space-adjacent blogs to show buzz. Drag the story out with "How We Did This" follow-ups.

## $1k / 1-week test
$0 testing is feasible. Pick 3 milestones, write 5–10 personalized pitches to small/mid blogs in your niche, sign up for HARO, follow 20 reporters on Twitter and engage. The "cost" is mostly time. **Skip PR firms for testing** — *"many of the (print) reporters we talked to said they ignore almost all pitches from PR firms but do listen to most founders."*

## Key metrics
- Coverage counts at each tier
- Referral traffic from each piece
- Downstream signups from each piece
- "As seen on" social proof for fundraising/sales
- Branded search lift in the week after coverage

PR is one of the single most effective things for **pushing investors over the edge** — they're already gambling and need social proof to justify the decision.

## Pitfalls
- **Pitching the wrong tier** (CNN before HN).
- **No angle** — making readers feel something, not informed.
- **"Wall of text" emails.**
- **Pitching without a milestone.**
- **Hiring a PR firm to *test* the channel** (expensive and ignored).
