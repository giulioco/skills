# Search Engine Marketing (SEM)

## Definition
Paid placement on search engines (primarily Google AdWords; also Microsoft Ad Center serving Yahoo/Bing/DuckDuckGo). Pay-per-click on keyword searches.

## When it works
- Any stage. **Especially valuable pre-product** as a product/messaging discovery tool — drive traffic to landing-page variants and let click-through and conversion data tell you what users actually want.
- Direct-response businesses where the buyer is searching with intent (**demand harvesting**).
- When CPA × LTV math works out and there's room to compress CPA over time.

## When it doesn't
- When CPA exceeds LTV with no realistic path to compression.
- When there's **no existing search demand** for your category (e.g., early Uber: nobody searched "alternatives to taxi cabs I can hire via my phone").

## Tactics

1. **Keyword research** with Google Keyword Planner; spy on competitors via KeywordSpy, SEMrush, SpyFu.
2. **Long-tail keywords** ("1990 Philadelphia census data" vs "census data") — cheaper, less competitive, more profitable per click.
3. **Start with just 4 ads** (Matthew Monahan's advice). Enough to baseline messaging, demographics, landing pages.
4. **Group keywords into ad groups** (one per product line). Title catchy + keyword + clear CTA. Include keyword in ad body.
5. **Use Google Analytics URL Builder** for unique tracked URLs per ad — track conversions, not just clicks.
6. **A/B test landing pages** with Optimizely or Visual Website Optimizer.
7. **Quality Score management:** CTR is the dominant input. Average AdWords CTR ≈ 2.0%; Google penalizes ads <1.5%. Use Dynamic Keyword Insertion to boost relevance.
8. **Advanced:** Content Network (display), Retargeting (3–10× higher CTRs), Conversion Optimizer, Negative Keywords, Scripts.

## $1k / 1-week test

1. Pick 10–20 long-tail keywords across 2–4 ad groups.
2. Build 4 ads testing distinct value propositions.
3. Point each at a unique landing page.
4. Spend $500–800 on clicks, hold $200 for landing-page tweaks.
5. Measure CTR, CPC, CPA, and the implied LTV-to-CAC trajectory.

**Goal:** rough read on whether the channel could work, not optimization.

## Key metrics
- **CTR** (% impressions → clicks)
- **CPC** (cost per click)
- **CPA** = CPC ÷ conversion rate
- **Quality Score**
- **Conversion percentage**
- Target: campaign trending to breakeven within a few weeks.

## Pitfalls
- **Don't expect day-1 profitability.**
- **Don't run only on the head term** — long-tail is where the margin lives.
- **Don't ignore Quality Score** — low CTR raises your CPC.
- **Don't optimize too early** with Conversion Optimizer (needs data).
- **Watch for irrelevant traffic** (e.g. ecommerce ranking on "free" terms).

## Pre-product validation play (Inflection / Archives.com)

Matthew Monahan ran landing-page tests *before* building product:
- Phase 1: "get access to census data" vs "get access to your family genealogy"
- Phase 2: "discover as many of your ancestors" vs "trace your family tree as far back as possible" vs "find out if you're related to a celebrity"

Used CTR differences across hundreds of thousands of impressions to decide product direction. Started with ~100,000 keywords, optimized to ~50,000 profitable ones, scaled to **$100k+/month**. Acquired by Ancestry for **$100M**.

## Companies + their plays
- **Inflection / Archives.com** — pre-product SEM as message discovery.
- **Most consumer SaaS** uses SEM as a baseline channel.
