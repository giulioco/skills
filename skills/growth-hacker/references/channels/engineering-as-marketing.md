# Engineering as Marketing

## Definition
Using engineering resources to build **free tools, calculators, widgets, and educational micro-sites** that draw and qualify your target customers. Treats software as a marketing *asset* with compounding returns vs. ads which stop the moment you stop paying.

## When it works
- Whenever your engineering capacity is underused for marketing.
- Your customers have a recurring informational pain you can solve in a focused tool.
- **Especially strong for B2B SaaS** where the tool's user *is* the buyer persona.

## When it doesn't
- The tool is unrelated to your core product (it draws the wrong leads).
- The tool is hard to use — friction kills word-of-mouth.
- You don't capture email or route to sales.

## Tactics

1. **Standalone free tools.** HubSpot's Marketing Grader (formerly Website Grader) — built in a few days because Dharmesh Shah was tired of manually checking prospect sites. **>3M sites have used it. Accounts for a large portion of HubSpot's 50,000+ leads/month.**

2. **Tools that double as qualification.** Anyone running it on their site is signaling intent.

3. **Niche tools complementing your product.** Moz's **Followerwonk** (Twitter analytics) and **Open Site Explorer** (link analysis). Both directly mirror needs of a Moz buyer.

4. **WP Engine speed test** at speed.wpengine.com — email required for the report → 7-email mini-course → upsell to premium hosting.

5. **Annual/seasonal micro-sites.** Codecademy's **Code Year** (2012) — weekly email lessons → **450,000 signups, nearly doubled userbase.** Patrick McKenzie's holiday-themed Bingo Card Creator micro-sites get reused yearly.

6. **Issue micro-sites.** Gabriel built **DontTrack.us** to expose Google's tracking and route awareness to DuckDuckGo. Resurges with every NSA news cycle and Data Privacy Day. DuckDuckGo now has 4 such micro-sites.

7. **Embeddable widgets.** Delicious bookmark widget more than tripled adoption (Chris Fralic). Facebook/Twitter share buttons are this pattern at scale.

8. **Domain-named tools for SEO.** RJMetrics owns **cohortanalysis.com** and **querymongo.com**; querymongo (SQL-to-MongoDB translator) is their highest-traffic micro-site, drives **hundreds of leads/month**.

## $1k / 1-week test

1. Spec the smallest possible tool that solves *one* recurring question your prospect has.
2. Build a v1 over a weekend or with a $500 freelancer.
3. Stand it up on a keyword-rich domain.
4. Capture email in exchange for the result.
5. Promote via your blog + 1 niche subreddit + Hacker News.
6. Measure email captures + qualified leads.

## Key metrics
- Tool sessions
- Email captures
- Leads sourced from the tool
- Sales attributed to tool-originated leads
- Organic search traffic to the tool's domain
- Embed/share count for widgets

## Pitfalls
- **Building a tool too far from your core product** (wrong leads).
- **Skipping email capture.**
- **Over-engineering before validating** that prospects actually want the output.
- **Forgetting to optimize the tool's domain for SEO.**
- **Building once and never iterating.** The 11th HubSpot Sidekick experiment was the one that worked (see `activation.md`).
