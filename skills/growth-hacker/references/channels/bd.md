# Business Development (BD)

## Definition
*"Sales with one key distinction: it's primarily focused on exchanging value through partnerships, whereas sales primarily focuses on exchanging dollars for a product."*

Five partnership types: **Standard, Joint Venture, Licensing, Distribution, Supply.**

## When it works
- You can find an **asset/audience the partner needs** and you have what big slow companies don't (focus, tech, content).
- Phase II/III when you have something credible to trade.

## When it doesn't
- Without strategic clarity — most BD deals fail; many startups waste cycles chasing brand-name logos with no fit.
- Phase I — you usually don't have leverage yet.

## Tactics

1. **Strategic anchor question (Chris Fralic, First Round):** *"What do you want your fundraising deck to look like in 12 months?"* Work backwards from that to the 3–5 partnerships needed.

2. **Pipeline spreadsheet (Charlie O'Donnell, Brooklyn Bridge):** Columns: Company, Partner Type (Publisher/Carrier/Reseller/etc.), Contact, Size, Relevance, Ease of Use, subjective priority. **Aim for 50–100 names.** *"If you go in and impress the top 50 folks in your space, it makes it that much harder for a competitor to get a deal done."*

3. **Bucket by attribute, not name (Fralic):** Don't chase "Condé Nast"; chase "internet retailers ranked 50–250 on the IR 500 with a director of ecommerce."

4. **Inventory-style field research (Fralic at Half.com):** Flew to Atlanta, worked a day in a used bookstore to learn how unsold inventory accumulated — that became the supply partnership template.

5. **One-page term sheets.** Both Fralic and Brenda Spoonemore (ex-NBA SVP) push for one-pagers, no lawyers if avoidable.

6. **Land traditional then go low-touch.** Delicious did high-touch deals with Washington Post + Mozilla *first*, then opened a public API once demand was proven. SlideShare embeds, Disqus install snippet, SoundCloud free embeds = same pattern.

7. **Custom-build for the partner.** Delicious built Washington Post a posting interface so Post IT didn't have to lift a finger.

8. **"How the deal was done" memo** after each close — milestones, sticking points, what unlocked it — to learn your own process.

## $1k / 1-week test

1. Build the **50-name pipeline**.
2. Get **investors/advisors to warm-intro 10**.
3. Pitch a one-page proposal.
4. Goal: **1 piloted deal or clear "no" pattern within a week.**

## Key metrics
- # deals in pipeline
- # warm intros made
- Time-to-meeting
- Deal close rate (assume most fail)
- Users/revenue per closed deal

## Pitfalls
- **Chasing logos** instead of fit.
- **Ignoring the partner's incentives.**
- **Over-lawyered terms** that kill momentum.
- **Assuming an API alone draws partners** (Delicious shows you need traditional landings first).
- **No memo per closed deal** = no codified BD process.
