# Sales

## Definition
Generating, qualifying, converting leads into paying customers, with a repeatable funnel.

## When it works
- **High-priced products needing hand-holding** (typically enterprise, $5k+ ACV).
- B2B with long evaluation cycles.
- Phase II/III for B2B SaaS.

## When it doesn't
- Self-serve consumer products.
- Low ACV products where sales cost > LTV.
- **As your top-of-funnel engine.** David Skok argues sales is *too expensive* as the lead-generation engine — use marketing for top-of-funnel and sales as a conversion tool.

## Tactics

### The "lunch pitch" (Sean Murphy, SKMurphy)
One page, 5–10 bullets + a visual. Use it to keep early conversations focused on the prospect's problem, not your product.

### Free consulting study (John Raguin, Guidewire)
Spent **7–10 days inside 40 insurance companies** producing a peer benchmark in exchange for honest product feedback. Zero-pressure entry that doubled as customer development.

### SPIN selling (Neil Rackham; based on 35,000 calls)
Four question types in order:
- **Situation** (use sparingly)
- **Problem** (use sparingly)
- **Implication** — makes the pain bigger
- **Need-payoff** — focuses on your solution

Ask 1–2 of each. Not a script — a structure.

### PNAME qualifier (Todd Vollmer)
- **Process** (how do they make decisions?)
- **Need** (what's the pain?)
- **Authority** (can this person decide?)
- **Money** (budget exists?)
- **Estimated Timing** (when?)

End every call with a follow-up email and a closing email asking *"will you agree to this closing timeline?"*

### Target one or two levels below the top (mid-size orgs)
CEO only for very small businesses (Sean Murphy). CEOs delegate down; start where the actual evaluator sits.

### A/B/C deal buckets (Mark Suster, Upfront)
- **A** = closes <3 months → **66–75% of rep time**
- **B** = 3–12 months → remainder
- **C** = >12 months → marketing owns them, not sales

### "To arm and to aim"
Marketing's job is to give reps **collateral** (decks, ROI calcs) and to point them at the **right targets**.

### Lead-nurturing back into pipeline (JBoss case)
Gave away docs (was paid before) → **10k leads/month** → Eloqua scoring on which pages prospects hit → marketing-team callouts → inside sales close at **~25% (industry 7–10%)** → average deal $10k+, **$65M revenue in 2 years**, $350M Red Hat acquisition.

### Reduce blockages
- SaaS (no IT install)
- Free trials
- Channel partners
- Demos
- FAQs
- References
- Email education
- Webinars
- Ease-of-use
- **Low intro price** (<$250/mo SMB, <$10k enterprise)
- Eliminate committee decisions

### Design from the customer's standpoint inward (Skok)
Ask: *"Am I sure this is the best product? Am I sure it works for my situation? ROI? Integration?"* Build sales materials that answer each.

## $1k / 1-week test
1. Build the **lunch-pitch one-pager**.
2. Cold-call/email **~50 buyers one or two levels down**.
3. Run SPIN script.
4. Qualify with PNAME.
5. **Goal:** 1 paid pilot or 5 LOIs.

## Key metrics
- Funnel stage conversion
- Blockage points (where deals stall)
- Deal size
- Sales cycle length
- A/B/C distribution
- Close rate

## Pitfalls
- **Wrong first customer** (Murphy's two traps):
  1. *"We'd love to learn about this technology"* tire-kicker who'll never buy.
  2. Self-styled *"change agent"* with no track record of pushing tech through.

  Either way you waste cycles giving free consulting.
- **Using sales as top-of-funnel** when marketing should be feeding it.
- **Not qualifying ruthlessly** — a "maybe" deal in the pipeline is a "no" wearing a costume.

## Companies + their plays
- **Guidewire** — free consulting study landed 40 insurance customers.
- **JBoss** — docs-for-email lead engine + Eloqua + inside sales (25% close rate).
- **HubSpot** — lead scoring on company size — Etsy/eBay micro-businesses get less rep time.
