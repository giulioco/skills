# Affiliate Programs

## Definition
You pay people/companies for performing defined actions — sale, qualified lead, signup. Three big segments: **Retail (~$2B/yr), Information Products, Lead Generation ($26B/yr).**

## When it works
- You know your **acceptable cost-per-acquisition** and your margins absorb it.
- Particularly strong in **finance, dating, web hosting, weight loss, poker, insurance** — anywhere customer LTV is high.
- Phase II/III after you've validated the funnel.

## When it doesn't
- Low-margin products.
- Unproven funnels (you don't know your CAC ceiling yet).
- Brand-sensitive products (affiliates may use sketchy tactics).

## Tactics

### Use an existing network rather than building
- **Commission Junction** (~$2k+ to join, premium retailers)
- **ClickBank** ($50 to list, digital products, **up to 75% commissions**)
- **Pepperjam** ($1k)
- **ShareASale** (~$500)
- **Affiliate.com** (strict approval)
- **Adknowledge, MobAff** (mobile)
- **Neverblue** ($20k+/mo spenders)
- **Clickbooth**
- **WhaleShark Media** (RetailMeNot/Deals2Buy)

### Affiliate types in retail
- **Coupon/deal sites** (RetailMeNot, CouponCabin, BradsDeals, Slickdeals)
- **Loyalty programs** (Upromise, Ebates)
- **Aggregators** (Nextag, PriceGrabber)
- **Email-list curators**
- **Vertical bloggers**

### Recruit your own users first
They're cheapest and warmest. Then content creators, bloggers, social influencers, email-list curators.

### Maneesh Sethi pattern (HackTheSystem)
Used the SEO product → asked for a commission deal → emailed his list with a **personal endorsement** → **~$30k in 2 years** from one offer. Also referred 3,000+ users to RescueTime.

### Compensation simplicity
Start with **flat fee per conversion** or **% of sale**. Mature into segmented payouts (eBay coupon codes per category) and tiered % rewards for top affiliates.

### Use product features as currency
**Freemium startups can pay affiliates with extended subscriptions or upgraded features instead of cash** (Dropbox referral storage; QuiBids free bids).

### Risk advantage over PPC (Kris Jones)
*"With affiliate marketing, the advertiser doesn't assume the risk. If the credit card declines, commission is zero. If a lead doesn't meet your rules, you don't pay."*

## $1k / 1-week test
1. **ShareASale** ($500) or **ClickBank** ($50) listing.
2. Recruit **5 existing power users** + **5 niche bloggers** with a flat-fee CPA.
3. Target: **50 conversions, validate payback.**

## Key metrics
- **CPA per affiliate type**
- Conversion %
- Share of revenue from top 10% of affiliates
- Lead-quality reject rate
- Refund rate (high refund = bad-fit traffic)

## Pitfalls
- **Building your own program first** (eats cash and engineering — use existing networks).
- **Paying out before you know LTV.**
- **Weak rules letting affiliates submit junk leads** — define quality criteria upfront.
- **Brand control** — affiliates may use tactics (spam, pop-ups, false claims) that damage your brand. Specify what's not allowed.

## Companies + their plays
- **Amazon** — 4–8.5% scaling commissions, run own program.
- **eBay/Pepperjam** — in-house network.
- **Netflix** — own program, refined CPA.
- **RescueTime** — Maneesh Sethi as top affiliate.
- **Half.com** — early launch program.
- **Dropbox / QuiBids** — in-product referral currency (storage, bids).
