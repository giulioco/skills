# Email Marketing

## Definition
Permission-based messaging at every stage of the customer lifecycle — acquisition, activation, retention, revenue, referrals. Includes broadcast, lifecycle/transactional ("drip"), and behavioral retargeting.

## When it works
- **Every stage.** *"Most effective way to universally reach people who have expressed interest"* (Colin Nederkoorn, Customer.io).
- Especially powerful when **personalized and tied to user actions**.
- The highest-LTV channel for most companies — Patrick McKenzie's email subscribers were **70× more likely to buy** his courses than blog/SEO/targeted-blog traffic.

## When it doesn't
- When you spam unsolicited bought lists — kills deliverability and brand.
- When templates feel robotic and impersonal.
- When you don't tie email to product behavior (broadcast-only treatment leaves leverage on the table).

## Tactics by lifecycle stage

### Acquisition
- Capture emails at the bottom of every blog post and landing page (Unbounce's biggest list-growth lever).
- Free mini-courses gated by email.
- Premium content (videos, whitepapers) gated by email.
- Advertise on complementary newsletters.

### Activation lifecycle emails
- **Dropbox emails users who signed up but haven't uploaded a file.**
- Define the *absolutely necessary* steps to value, then trigger an email each time a user fails to advance.
- **Twitter found users following ≥5 people stayed engaged**; redesigned signup around that threshold and triggered emails for users below it.
- **30-minute personal email** (Colin Nederkoorn): every Customer.io signup gets an automated email from his real address: *"Hey {{first_name}}, I'm Colin, CEO of Customer.io. I wanted to reach out to see if you need any help getting started."* **17% reply rate.**

### Retention
- **Mint's weekly financial summary.**
- BillGuard's monthly card report.
- Dating sites surfacing unread messages.
- **"You are so awesome" emails** (Brennan Dunn at Planscope sends users their weekly earnings).

### Revenue
- WP Engine's 7-email mini-course on WordPress speed → premium hosting pitch.
- Cart-abandonment retargeting.
- Freemium feature gates ("upgrade to premium → more dates").
- Usage-cap nudges (Skype credits running low).

### Referrals
- **Dropbox's invite-for-storage** built on email.
- Groupon's "needs N buyers to unlock" deal mechanic.
- Referrals via email convert **far better than social mentions**.

## Tooling
- **Deliverability:** MailChimp, Constant Contact, ConvertKit.
- **Behavioral / lifecycle:** Vero, Customer.io, Klaviyo, ActiveCampaign.
- **Never use a no-reply address** — surface a person and let users reply.

## Test everything

- Subjects, formats, images, send time.
- **9am–12pm in customer's time zone** is a common starting point.
- Sending at the original signup time of day is another good test.

## $1k / 1-week test

Set up 3 lifecycle emails:

1. **Welcome with personal reply-to from a founder** (Nederkoorn pattern).
2. **Day-3 nudge to inactive signups** based on the activation step they skipped.
3. **Day-7 "are you stuck?" email** to non-activated.

Run for 1 week. Measure open rate, reply rate, **activation lift vs. cohort that didn't get the sequence**.

## Key metrics
- Deliverability
- Open rate
- Click rate
- Reply rate (most underrated — replies are 100× engagement)
- Activation rate
- Retention curve lift
- Revenue per email
- List growth rate
- Unsubscribe rate

## Pitfalls
- **Buying lists.**
- **Generic copy** — Copyhackers-grade copywriting moves the needle dramatically.
- **Not tying email to product behavior.**
- **Treating email as broadcast-only** when the highest-leverage email is triggered, personalized lifecycle.
- **No-reply addresses** — kill the relationship potential.
- **Assuming "unsubscribe rate" is the only health metric** — open rate trend matters more.

## Companies + their plays
- **Dropbox** — referral-for-storage email + activation reminder emails.
- **Twitter** — 5-follow activation threshold + retention alerts.
- **Customer.io / Colin Nederkoorn** — the "Help getting started?" pattern (17% reply rate).
- **Patrick McKenzie** — email subscribers 70× more likely to buy.
- **Groupon, Fab, JackThreads, Thrillist, Zappos** — email as primary traction channel via deals/discounts/newsletters.
- **WP Engine** — 7-email drip → premium hosting upsell.
- **Mint** — weekly financial summary as retention engine.
