# Existing Platforms

## Definition
Sites/apps/networks with **hundreds of millions of users** (App Stores, Mozilla/Chrome extensions, Facebook, Twitter, Pinterest, Tumblr, Snapchat, TikTok, Vine, Amazon, eBay, Craigslist, GitHub, Behance) you can leverage for distribution.

## When it works
- The platform is **rapidly growing and uncrowded**.
- You can fill a real **platform gap**.
- The platform's **editors actively curate** and you can be excellent enough to get featured.

## When it doesn't
- After the platform saturates (early-platform arbitrage windows close).
- For products with no native viral hook on the platform.
- When the platform is hostile to your category (alcohol on FB Ads, etc.).

## Tactics

### Trainyard ranking flywheel (Mark Johnson, Focused Apps)
Free version released → glowing post on a popular Italian blog → **#1 free in Italy in a day (22k DLs) → top in UK (450k DLs in a week) → Apple feature on day 7.**

The 8-step loop: ads → charts → organic → higher charts → friends tell friends → repeat.

### Buy-into-charts services
AdMob ads, Tapjoy installs, FreeAppADay, cross-promotion networks, in-house cross-promotion.

### Build-early strategy (Evernote / Phil Libin)
*"We killed ourselves to be in every App Store launch on day one."* Day-one iPhone, iPad (custom-designed), Android, Kindle Fire — got featured **for weeks at a time**. Failed on Nokia/Windows/BlackBerry, but Android wins covered the misses.

### Multiple-app strategy (Evernote)
Released Evernote Food, Evernote Hello, **Evernote Peek** (iPad Smart Cover study tool — Apple featured it in a commercial; **#1 Education for a month; 500k new users from Peek alone**). The brand occupies multiple App Store categories.

### Special-features strategy (Alex Pachikov)
Ask: *"What would Apple/Google/Microsoft want to feature?"* Then build that. Editors are looking for content that makes their platform look good.

### Fill the platform gap
- **YouTube** → MySpace's missing video host (and pulled MySpace users back to YouTube via embeds).
- **Bit.ly** → Twitter URL shortening.
- **Imgur** → reddit image hosting.
- **AirBnB** → Craigslist's cross-post button drove tens of thousands of bookings.
- **PayPal** bought goods on eBay and required PayPal payment — bootstrapped a payment standard.

### Browser extensions
**Evernote saw web usage jump 205%** from launching extensions when they already had 6M users.

## $1k / 1-week test

1. Pick **one emerging platform** (TikTok, Discord, ChatGPT plugin store, etc.).
2. Ship a **minimum-viable integration** that fills one missing feature.
3. Submit to editors with a **feature pitch** ("here's what your users would love").
4. Track installs/engagement vs. estimated ROI.

## Key metrics
- Rank in store/category
- **Feature placement** (the holy grail)
- Ratings (matter for editors and users)
- Downloads/day
- Retention from platform-acquired users (often lower than other channels — track carefully)

## Pitfalls
- **Waiting for a platform to be "proven"** — by then it's crowded and discovery barriers are high.
- **Chris Dixon:** *"Bet early, accept failure when the platform fails. Some of the most successful startups grew by making bets on emerging platforms that were not yet saturated and where barriers to discovery were low. Betting on new platforms means you'll likely fail if the platform fails, but it also dramatically lowers the distribution risks."*
- **Treating App Store ranks as a one-shot push** instead of a sustained quality-rating game.
- **Forgetting platform retention curves** — App Store-acquired users often retain worse than direct.
