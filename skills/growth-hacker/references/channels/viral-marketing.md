# Viral Marketing (as a channel)

For the deep philosophy and engineering of virality, see `../virality.md`. This file covers virality specifically as one of the 19 traction channels — the testing, comparison, and decision criteria for choosing it as your inner-circle channel.

## Definition
Getting existing users to refer others. *"In the context of startups, going viral means that every user you acquire brings in at least one other user."*

## When it works
- Consumer products on **super-platforms** (Facebook, email, App Stores, Instagram, Snapchat, Pinterest).
- Products where the user **genuinely wants** to spread it.
- Products that get *better* the more people use them (network effects).
- **Phase I/II** is sweet spot — viral compounds, so early establishment pays off.

## When it doesn't
- Products that aren't inherently viral.
- Low-value products (sharing costs social capital — payoff must exceed cost).
- B2B with long sales cycles (the buyer isn't the sharer).
- Andrew Chen: even expert teams need **1–2 engineers working 2–3 months minimum** to ship a viral channel.

## Six viral loop types
| Type | Example |
|---|---|
| **Word of mouth** | Facebook in college dorms |
| **Inherent virality** | Skype, WhatsApp, Snapchat |
| **Collaboration** | Google Docs, Figma |
| **Communications embed** | Hotmail, "Sent from my iPhone" |
| **Incentive** | Dropbox referral storage |
| **Embedded buttons / broadcast** | YouTube embed code, Spotify scrobbling |

## Viral math
```
K = i × c   (K = viral coefficient; i = invites/user; c = conversion %)
```
- **K > 1** = exponential growth.
- **K > 0.5** = significantly amplifies your other efforts.
- Shortening **viral cycle time** is one of the first things to focus on — a K of 1.2 with a 1-day cycle outperforms K of 1.5 with a 7-day cycle within months.

## Tactics
1. Pick a distribution platform "**not too old, not too new** — in the middle."
2. Map the entire loop, cut unnecessary steps, increase invite mechanisms.
3. Invitations: short, succinct, personal-feeling.
4. Conversion pages should mirror invitation messaging.
5. Test 5–10× changes first (new auto-responder sequence, new website design, new onboarding), then optimize.
6. Calculate K for **subgroups** — "viral pockets" can be taking off while aggregate is flat.
7. **Seed** the loop with SEO/ads at the top. Loops aren't self-sustaining.
8. **Andrew Chen's beginner advice:** copy someone else's loop verbatim until yours works similarly.

## $1k / 1-week test

Realistic warning: full viral implementation is 2–3 months of engineering. The 1-week test means:

1. Implement a **minimal invite mechanic** (single share link with attribution).
2. Set up tracking dashboard (invites sent, conversions, K, cycle time).
3. Seed via existing channel (existing users, blog, ads).
4. Measure baseline K and cycle time within a week.
5. Decide: is K plausibly trending toward 0.3+ at 5–10× this scale?

## Key metrics
- **K** (viral coefficient)
- **Viral cycle time**
- Invites sent per user
- Invite-to-signup conversion
- Cohort growth rate from viral vs. paid

## Pitfalls (Andrew Chen)
- **Non-inherently viral products bolting on viral features** (tax software with share buttons).
- **Bad products trying to go viral** — virality amplifies the product, including bad reviews.
- **Not enough A/B tests** (1–3 of every 10 viral experiments yield positive results).
- **Not understanding how users currently share** before adding "best practice" Facebook Like buttons.
- **Skipping coaching from people who've done it.**
- **Treating virality as a tactic rather than a deep product strategy.**
- **Branchout cautionary tale:** virality without aha = high churn = wasted growth (4M → 25M → collapse).

## Companies + their plays
- **Dropbox** — incentive: extra space for referring friends. Built directly into product after SEM math failed.
- **Hotmail** — communications embed in email signature.
- **YouTube** — embedded video widget + minutes-long cycle time.
- **Facebook** — word-of-mouth in college dorms first, then explicit hooks.
- **Skype/WhatsApp/Snapchat** — inherent virality (worthless without contacts).
- **Google Docs** — collaboration loop.
- **AirBnB, Uber, PayPal, Gilt** — referral credits.
- **Pinterest, Instagram** — leveraged super-platforms (Facebook feed) → tens of millions in <3 years.
- **myZamana** — action-triggered emails, 100k → 4M in <1 year.
