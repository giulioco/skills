## Video Format Templates

### Format 1: Shocked Face / Hand Over Mouth

**Structure**:
1. Creator with genuinely shocked/horrified/amazed expression
2. Hand over mouth or jaw drop reaction
3. Long text hook overlay
4. Cut to immediate screen recording of app

**Duration**: 8-15 seconds

**Best For**: Travel apps, utility apps, discovery moments

**Hook Integration**: Text explains what shocked them

**Examples**:
- Flight deal apps (Triips, Roamy)
- "I could KISS the flight attendant" format
- Close-up UGC = top-performing variation

**Pro Tip**: Airplane/airport locations = trust signals

### Format 2: The 6-Second Loop

**Structure**:
1. 6-second video
2. Text overlay that takes >6 seconds to read
3. Forces viewer to loop
4. No audio or subtle background music

**Duration**: Exactly 6 seconds

**Best For**: High bookmark/save rates, quote content, list content

**Examples**:
- Candle: Multiple 4-7M view videos using this format
- Wall-of-text Gen Z relationship advice
- Numbered lists that extend beyond 6 seconds

**Metrics**: High save rate drives algorithmic distribution

### Format 3: Lecture Hall / Angry Professor

**Structure**:
1. POV of student in lecture hall/classroom
2. Professor (actor) yelling or catching student
3. "You are using [App]!" or "I told you NOT to use [App]!"
4. Cut to app demo or student defending

**Duration**: 15-45 seconds

**Best For**: Study apps, AI tools, academic productivity

**Variations**:
- Angry professor argument (SaveMyGPA: 8.9M views, 41 sec)
- Professor catches student cheating with AI
- Cooking in classroom wild stunts (MindGrasp)
- Drinking gallons of milk for attention

**Custom Domains**: savemygpa.com, ihatereading.com for organic mention

**Examples**:
- **TwainGPT**: 197M views with pure AI content
- **Save My GPA**: 8.9M views single video
- **MindGrasp**: 133M views from 1,076 videos, hired actors to squat in lecture halls

### Format 4: Cheating/Stalking Hook

**Structure**:
1. Emotional setup: suspicion or discovery
2. Story of catching partner or finding secrets
3. App reveal as the tool that uncovered truth
4. Crying, shocked, or angry reaction

**Duration**: 15-60 seconds (can be longer for story time)

**Best For**: Dating apps, location trackers, social search tools

**Psychological Elements**:
- Fear-based curiosity
- Relationship paranoia
- Social proof through comments

**Examples**:
- **TruthSeek**: 350M views using emotional car b-roll with cheating hooks
- **Tea Dating**: 110M views, "45 minutes til he gets home" format
- **The Ick**: Stalking angle, "YEARS of stalking" hook (8.1M views)
- **Cheaterbuster**: "POV: shopping for his suit and find his tinder" (3.1M views)

**Comment Engineering**: Plant controversial details (criminal records, "dog" drama) to spark debate

### Format 5: Before/After Transformation

**Structure**:
1. "Before" state: struggling, frustrated, unfit
2. Transition moment
3. "After" state: thriving, successful, transformed
4. App credited in middle or end

**Duration**: 10-20 seconds

**Best For**: Fitness apps, language learning, productivity, beauty

**Variations**:
- **Two-slide carousel**: First slide before, second slide after
- **Video**: Side-by-side split screen
- **AI-generated**: Easier to scale, adds humor element

**Examples**:
- Language learning progress over weeks
- Fitness transformation (Tone AI, Liftoff)
- Academic improvement (F to A student)

### Format 6: Fake Podcast / Interview

**Structure**:
1. Two people in conversation (often scripted to feel organic)
2. One shares "secret" or "crazy story"
3. Natural segue to app mention
4. Feels like eavesdropping

**Duration**: 30-90 seconds

**Best For**: Storytelling, complex value props, building credibility

**Why It Works**: High retention, conversation format feels authentic

**Variations**:
- Real podcast clips (clipping strategy)
- Fake podcast setup (talking head with guest)
- Self-podcast (solo talking head in podcast aesthetic)

**Examples**:
- Fake/Self Podcast: 48M views across 387 clips
- Build authority persona while promoting

### Format 7: POV Scenario

**Structure**:
1. "POV:" hook establishes scenario
2. Act out the scenario (or show screen recording)
3. App appears as solution to scenario problem
4. Often includes transition or reveal

**Duration**: 10-30 seconds

**Best For**: Relatable moments, utility apps, social features

**Scenarios**:
- POV: Robbery (fake scenario revealing lock screen widget - 12.3M views)
- POV: 15 minutes to make dinner (21M views - Mob)
- POV: Finding boyfriend's Tinder (1.4M views - Cheaterbuster)

### Format 8: Wall-of-Text Silent Video

**Structure**:
1. 6-10 second video (often static or slow motion)
2. Long text overlay filling screen
3. Silent or subtle music
4. Requires loop to read fully

**Duration**: 6-10 seconds

**Best For**: Quote content, advice, confessions, hot takes

**Text Characteristics**:
- All caps for emphasis
- Multiple emojis
- Gen Z slang and linguistic markers
- Run-on sentences

**Examples**:
- Candle app relationship advice
- Time Atlas philosophical content
- Fitdrop motivational quotes

**Metrics**: Forces loops, high bookmark rates

### Format 9: Day-in-the-Life / Vlog Style

**Structure**:
1. "Day X of..." or timestamp framing
2. Casual filming (car, eating, getting ready)
3. Natural app integration mid-activity
4. Authentic, unpolished feel

**Duration**: 15-45 seconds

**Best For**: Lifestyle apps, habit trackers, daily-use products

**Variations**:
- Trading day-in-life (Chart AI: 40M+ views)
- Study routine with app
- Cooking/meal prep featuring recipe app
- Morning routine with productivity app

**Examples**:
- Stock trading live format
- Build in public journey
- Language learning progress updates

### Format 10: Green Screen Product Roasting

**Structure**:
1. Founder or creator in front of green screen
2. Product image/video in background
3. Roasting or analyzing the product
4. Educational + entertaining tone

**Duration**: 30-60 seconds

**Best For**: Health scanners, product analysis apps, educational tools

**Why It Works**: Authority positioning + entertainment value

**Examples**:
- **Oasis**: 120K followers, 50M+ views roasting water products
- Health scanner apps analyzing grocery items
- "Hot Finds" or "Avoid These" format

## Slideshow Formats

### Standard UGC Slideshow Structure

**Slide 1**: Creator photo/selfie + attention-grabbing hook
**Slides 2-5**: Tips, advice, or story progression
**Slide 3 or 4**: APP CTA (NEVER last slide - kills bookmarks)
**Final Slide**: Closing thought or call-back to hook

**Duration**: Each slide 2-3 seconds, total 10-20 seconds

**Best For**: Faceless scale, batch production, aesthetic content

### Format 1: "Ick or Stick" Polls

**Structure**:
- Dating app poll questions
- "This or that?" variations
- Triggering relationship questions
- Comment engagement focus

**Examples**:
- Cray Cray: 144M views on relationship question slideshows
- CoupleTok formats
- Friend group compatibility tests

### Format 2: Toxic Motivation Study Content

**Structure**:
- Aggressive/funny motivational quotes
- Study tips with edge
- Academic pressure themes
- September = "2nd chance at new year's" angle

**Target**: StudyTok, back-to-school season (August/September)

**Seasonality**: Peaks during exam periods

### Format 3: Ranking Format

**Structure**:
- Numbered list or tier list
- Workout splits, foods, beauty products, etc.
- App screenshot on slide revealing ranking feature
- Social proof through expertise

**Examples**:
- Symmetry fitness app ranking workout plans
- Beauty product tier lists
- Food/diet rankings

### Format 4: Educational Lists (Buzzfeed Style)

**Structure**:
- "X things that [benefit]"
- Each slide one item + explanation
- Satisfies curiosity
- App integration slide 3-4

**Examples**:
- "5 foods that make you taller" (Taller app)
- "7 study tips that saved my GPA"
- "10 travel hacks you need to know"

### Format 5: Long Story Slideshow (27+ Slides)

**Structure**:
- Lengthy narrative told across many slides
- Dramatic or controversial story
- App appears mid-story as part of narrative
- High completion despite length

**Duration**: Total 45-90 seconds

**Best For**: Compelling narratives, divorce/breakup stories, dramatic reveals

**Why It Works**: Investment in story â†’ completion â†’ curiosity about app

### Format 6: Pinterest/AI Image + Hook

**Structure**:
- Slide 1: Aesthetic Pinterest or AI-generated image + hook
- Slides 2-3: Creator tips or growth mindset advice
- Slide 4: Subtle product reveal "I use [App]"
- All faceless, easily scalable

**Production**:
- Download Pinterest images
- Run through GPT-4o for variations
- Use MoviePy to automate transitions
- Bulk export and schedule

**Examples**:
- **Go Viral**: 6-slide format, 10M+ views, $100K MRR
- Pinterest quote edits
- AI-generated aesthetic backgrounds

### Format 7: Before vs After Carousel

**Structure**:
- Two-slide format
- Funny, shocking, or inspiring transformation
- AI-generated images for humor

**Examples**:
- "1864 vs 2064" historical comparisons
- Fitness before/after
- Academic transformation (F student â†’ A student)

### Format 8: Tour My [Thing]

**Structure**:
- "Tour my savings" (Budget Bestie: 2.2M views)
- "Tour my routine"
- "Tour my meal plan"
- Each slide shows aspect + app screenshot integration

**Background**: Image + app screenshot per slide

**Why It Works**: Gives viewers inside look, builds trust through transparency

### Format 9: Fake UGC Illusion

**Structure**:
- Use Pinterest images to create consistent persona
- Example: @sophiainnyc0 (Left Field) - fake UGC with 14.33% engagement
- Appears to be real person, actually stock images
- Maintain consistent "character" across posts

**Ethics**: Gray area but highly effective

### Format 10: Christmas/Seasonal Hooks

**Structure**:
- Seasonal confession or story format
- Holiday-themed hooks
- Timely relevance

**Examples**:
- Cravr: 1.9M views with Christmas confession
- "Create group album for 2025" (Yope - New Year's)
- Back-to-school content surge
- "Wrapped" season variations (Spotify, TikTok, Travel)

## Faceless Content Formats

### Format 1: Screen Recording + Voiceover

**Structure**:
1. 30-60 second app demo
2. Voiceover explaining features
3. Hook text overlay at start
4. Clear feature showcase

**No Face Required**: Pure screen recording

**Best For**: Complex apps needing demonstration

**Variations**:
- Text-only (no voiceover)
- Instrumental background music
- Gen Z commentary style

### Format 2: Stock B-Roll + Text Hook

**Structure**:
1. 2-3 second emotional stock footage (crying, car, aesthetic)
2. Long text hook overlay
3. Cut to app screenshot or demo
4. Dramatic music or silence

**Examples**:
- **TruthSeek**: Emotional car/crying b-roll + relationship drama hooks (350M views)
- **Rayz**: Repurposed attractive content, POV Gen Z hooks (12.2M views, $80K MRR)

**Source**: Download content from others, resize to 2.5-second clips, add hooks

### Format 3: AI-Generated Avatars

**Structure**:
1. 2-3 second AI-generated creator intro (HeyGen, D-ID)
2. Cut to app demo screen recording
3. No voiceover (instrumental sound only)
4. Just enough "human feel" before suspicion sets in

**Examples**:
- **POV Camera**: $150K+ revenue, 130M views, 11 accounts, AI avatars
- **LingoLooper**: Mixed AI/real UGC
- AI animation biblical stories (Pray.com)

**Production**: Automate with templates, swap variables, bulk generate

### Format 4: AI Biblical/Historical Animations

**Structure**:
1. AI-generated biblical or mythical scenes
2. Modern twist: "What if Moses had an iPhone?" (350.9K views)
3. Religious/inspirational messaging
4. Faceless but high production value feel

**Best For**: Bible apps, history apps, educational content

**Engagement**: Rage comments ("AI disrespects the Bible") boost algorithm

**Examples**:
- **TwainGPT**: 197M views, pure AI, zero human creators
- Pray.com biblical animations (832M total views)

### Format 5: Meme-Style Graphics

**Structure**:
1. Wojak characters or meme templates
2. Relatable situation + app screenshot
3. Text overlay with meme format
4. Zero filming required

**Examples**:
- Endeavorise using Wojak character
- Relationship meme formats
- "Me vs My Brain" style memes

### Format 6: Comparison Videos

**Structure**:
1. Multiple app logos side-by-side
2. White background comparison
3. Features or benefits listed
4. YOUR logo must stand out visually

**Requirements**:
- Bold, distinctive logo essential
- Massive scale needed (30+ accounts) for ROI
- Only works if your logo is most eye-catching

**Best For**: AI companion apps, crowded categories

**Warning**: Requires volume to convert viewers

### Format 7: Black Screen Fade-In Lists

**Structure**:
1. Hook text on black screen
2. List items fade in sequentially
3. "Foods that will make you taller" format
4. Works for any niche topic

**Duration**: 15-30 seconds

**Production**: Extremely easy to batch create

**Examples**:
- Taller app
- Beauty tips
- Study hacks
- Relationship advice

### Format 8: Team Zoom Call

**Structure**:
1. Record laptop screen during Zoom with team/creators
2. Hook describes "tea" of what's happening
3. Captures authentic collaboration
4. Behind-the-scenes feel

**Best For**: Study, education, jobs, AI tools, dating/cheating scenarios

**Why It Works**: Voyeuristic appeal, authentic feel

### Format 9: Soft Eroticism Content

**Structure**:
1. Suggestive but not explicit imagery
2. Target: younger women demographic
3. Easy to clone format
4. High engagement

**Example**: 115M views across 84 videos in this category

**Note**: Content policy boundaries, risk of takedowns

### Format 10: Clipping Network

**Structure**:
1. Long-form content exists (podcast, YouTube)
2. Network of accounts clip and repost 15-30 sec segments
3. Hooks added to clips
4. Polaroid-frame re-edits to avoid duplication

**Hiring**: Pay clippers on CPM basis via Discord servers

**Examples**:
- **FxAlexG**: 7 accounts, 79M views
- 500+ different clipping accounts identified
- First accounts compound views over time

## UGC Formats

### Format 1: Classic UGC Structure

**Part 1 (0-4 seconds)**: UGC clip (dancing, lip syncing, shocked face, selfie)
**Part 2 (4-15 seconds)**: App demo directly screen-recorded

**Why It Works**: Hook feels organic, app sneaks in after you're already watching

**Examples**:
- Yope: 2-4 second UGC + app demo (207M views across 145 ambassadors)
- Standard across most ambassador programs

### Format 2: Selfie Vlog

**Structure**:
1. Creator speaking directly to camera
2. Shares personal experience with app
3. Shows app on phone screen
4. Authentic testimonial feel

**Duration**: 15-45 seconds

**Tone**: Conversational, unscripted (even if scripted)

**Best For**: High-trust conversions, personal recommendations

### Format 3: In-Action Storytelling

**Structure**:
1. Filming while doing activity (eating, makeup, car)
2. Story time format
3. Natural product integration mid-story
4. Roleplay transition to demo

**Examples**:
- **Spicy Cubes**: 39 accounts, 120M views, eating on camera ER/affair stories
- 3-minute story times with product mid-way
- "Close friends" aesthetic

**Duration**: 30 seconds to 3 minutes

### Format 4: Phone-in-Hand Demo

**Structure**:
1. 7-second intro holding phone
2. Flip phone to show screen
3. Navigate app features
4. Back to face for closing

**Duration**: 15-30 seconds

**Best For**: UI/UX showcase, feature-rich apps

### Format 5: Two-Slide Creator Slideshow

**Slide 1**: Creator mirror selfie + hook text
**Slide 2**: App screenshot

**Duration**: 6-10 seconds total (3-5 sec per slide)

**Examples**:
- **Howbout**: Female creator selfie, 1.5M views
- **Partiful**: 1.4M views
- **Go Viral**: 742K views

**Production**: Extremely fast to create, easy to scale across creators

### Format 6: Silent Facial Reaction

**Structure**:
1. Creator's face reacting to something on screen (off-camera)
2. Emotions: shock, excitement, laughter, tears
3. Text hook explains what they're reacting to
4. Cut to app demo showing the cause

**Duration**: 10-20 seconds

**Music**: Trending audio or silence

### Format 7: "Brother/Boyfriend Hook" Viral Loop

**Structure**:
1. "My [brother/boyfriend/friend] is gonna show you..."
2. Vouching for person adds trust
3. That person demonstrates app
4. Relationship dynamic creates curiosity

**Examples**:
- **BiteSight**: "My brother's gonna show you his app" (17.2M TikTok + 520K IG + 20M Twitter)
- "My boyfriend made this and you're gonna like it" (1.2M views)

**Why It Works**: Social proof through relationship endorsement

### Format 8: Lazy PowerPoint Method

**Structure**:
1. Simple, child-like PowerPoint presentation
2. Shameless self-promo but funny/relatable
3. "Normal person with project and dream" vibe
4. Slide through features with personality

**Best For**: Founders, authentic pitches, comedic angle

### Format 9: One Camera, Ten T-Shirts

**Batch Production Method**:
1. Book 1-2 hour calendar slot
2. Sit down with phone/camera
3. Pack 2-3 extra outfits
4. Record 2-3 clips in each outfit
5. Mix and match for weeks of "fresh" content

**Result**: 10+ videos from single session

**Examples**:
- **Couple Joy**: 12+ videos from single 30-minute shoot (pink shirt batch)
- Efficient creator management

### Format 10: Campus Interviews / Street Content

**Structure**:
1. Approach people on campus or street
2. Ask question related to app ("What's your GPA?")
3. Their reaction or answer
4. Transition to app demo or branding

**Examples**:
- **Turbolearn**: "What's ur GPA!?" street interviews (15M views)
- Campus-specific content
- Diversity in demographics adds reach

**Best For**: Study apps, location-based apps, demographic-specific products

## Content Testing Framework

### Hook A/B Testing Process

**Test Variables**:
1. Same actor, consistent format
2. Change ONLY the hook text
3. Post 10 different hook variations
4. Track: Views, engagement rate, "what's the app?" comments

**After 30 Videos**:
- Identify 3x median performer
- Make 30 more variations of that winning hook
- Test format variations while keeping hook
- Find new 3x performer
- Repeat cycle

### Format Performance Metrics

**Good Format Indicators**:
- High bookmark/save rate
- "What's the app?" comments
- Completion rate >50%
- 3x median views achieved

**Bad Format Indicators**:
- Low engagement despite views
- No question comments
- <30% completion rate
- Flat median across many videos

### Iteration Strategy

**Phase 1: Format Testing** (Videos 1-30)
- Try 5-10 different formats
- Same hook across formats
- Find what resonates

**Phase 2: Hook Testing** (Videos 31-60)
- Winning format from Phase 1
- 10+ different hooks
- Find 3x performer

**Phase 3: Scale** (Videos 61+)
- Winning format + winning hook
- Make 30+ variations
- Add to multiple accounts
- Geographic/language variants
